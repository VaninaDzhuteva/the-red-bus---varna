The Red Bus Mission Statement: Our aim is to provide young children with a secure foundation to English language learning. It is our goal to inspire a love of languages as well as promote independence in all children. Each lesson will include singing, early handwriting skills (penmanship) and Phonics. The Red Bus follows the Phonics International scheme alongside Jolly Phonics resources which has had a very positive impact on early reading skills in Jennifer's last school.

Lessons are taught thematically; a topic/book/or song is studied and explored in two week cycles. This allows time to learn and master new vocabulary and new language skills. Children will also have the opportunity to develop Practical Life skills through the medium of English. Each lesson is planned around the children. Each child is encouraged to work to the best of their ability and is not compared to peers or against chronological markers. Children are supported in the classroom to be independent; this would include learning about how to get their own resources, how to put things away, and how to share with other children.

The class room and the other rooms are furnished and sorted in a way so that they could make you feel like home. The children are feeling calm when they start their class, which is an advantage when you are learning a foreign language. The classes, when possible, are a mix of ages. This allows opportunity for young children to work alongside more able peers and allows older children to gain a sense of responsibility when assisting younger members of the class.

In this sense, our classes are very calm and an atmosphere of cooperation is encouraged. Jennifer has a deep interest in the Montessori method of teaching. A great emphasis is placed on phonics. Jennifer keeps up to date with the latest developments in the field of English language teaching and phonics. Developing good phonological awareness has shown to place children two chronological years ahead of their class peers in terms of reading, spelling and pronunciation.
